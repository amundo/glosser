Categories of Evidentiality

Examples from Eastern Pomo (McLendon 2003)

Evidence through sensation:

    1. bi·Yá
    2. hand
    1. pʰa·bé-kʰ-ink̓e
    2. burn-punc-sens-ev

I burned my hand

Speaker feels the sensation of burning

Evidence through inference:

    1. bé·k-al
    2. 3pl-patient
    1. pʰabé-k-ine
    2. burn-punc-infr

They must have gotten burned

I see circumstantial evidence — signs of a fire, bandages, burn cream

Evidence through "hearsay":

    1. bé·k-al
    2. 3pl-patient
    1. pʰabé-kʰ-·le
    2. burn-punc-hs

They got burned, they say

I am reporting what I was told

Evidence through direct experience:

    1. mí·-p-al
    2. 3sg-MASC-PNT
    1. pʰa·bé-k-a
    2. burn-dir-ev

He got burned

I have direct evidence
